Introduction & Course Content

Course Topics

  1. Logic of Bayesian Inference (with no informative prior, Bayesian estimates collapse to the MLE estimate)

–

  1. Probability model of uncertainty; propagating this uncertainty into substantive, model-based inference

–

  1. Focus on understanding the construction of distributions and how we might parameterize these

–

  1. Be Bayesian about data…

–

  1. Understand how data generating processes and the construction of measurement dictates, or bounds, modeling strategies

–

  1. Using as the core tool of analysis

Persistent Themes

  1. Attention to fit of the research design, and statistical procedures, to our research questions

–

  1. Attention to data generating process as the central guide to modeling strategies

–

  1. Conceptual understanding of the underlying math for MLE

–

  1. Model-based simulation in order to more fully understand statistical procedures, inference, and substantive implications of model findings

–

  1. Visualizing data—for descriptive purposes, to understand causal inference, the robustness of our models and measures, and the substantive impact of model findings; communicating research results to a general audience

–

  1. Applied data analysis — lots of Code

Vignettes & Research Paper

Content of the Vignettes

  • Vignettes should explain a method or statistical procedure in a way that a generalist in data science, analytics, or statistics can understand

–

  • Vignettes should contain code, but also use text to explain the chunks and lines of code - key difference between the vignettes and the research paper

–

  • Vignettes should use real data and cover all coding and statistical procedures necessary to procure or collect the data, clean the data, analyze the data, and communicate the results

–

  • Vignettes do not contain literature reviews, social science theory, or broad discussions of implications; they may illustrate inference however, or how to learn from data or an analytical procedure

–

  • See “Scraping, Downloading, and Storing PDFs in R” in Towards Data Science for an example of a vignette.

Content of the Research Paper

  • In general, the paper should look like a journal submission

–

  • The research design should map onto the research question, theory, and hypotheses; this is the most common reason for rejection at journals

–

  • The paper will contain the mathematical representation of the model and other statistical procedures

–

  • The paper will contain visual displays of quantitative information (VDQIs)—for both descriptive and causal inference

–

  • Serious effort to engage counterfactual scenarios and explanations

Structure of the Research Paper

  1. Research question and why it is important—no talk of “gap” filling.

–

  1. Theoretical (causal models) & conceptual development (measurement models).

–

  1. Data & Measures—justification of indicators and measures, noting potential measurement errors or conceptual and theoretical strengths.

–

  1. Hypotheses—map theoretical expectations and concepts directly onto measures.

–

  1. Methodological Approach—including mathematical and theoretical justification for statistical models and procedures.

–

  1. Findings—must contain discussion of robustness of approach and estimates.

–

  1. Discussion—must contain VDQIs and exploration of counterfactuals; emphasize substantive findings.

–

  1. Conclusion—what did we learn…theoretically, conceptually, methodologically…about the world.
